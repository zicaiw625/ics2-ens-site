Purpose

ICS EU ENS HS6 Add-on exists to help organizations prepare complete and accurate Entry Summary Declaration (ENS) datasets for the EU’s Import Control System 2 (ICS2). The add-on standardizes columns, validates critical fields (e.g., 6-digit HS codes and descriptive goods text), highlights gaps, and produces carrier-neutral CSV files—reducing rejections and turnaround time.

Structured templates

Creates master/house worksheets with ENS-aligned columns for transport mode, locations, parties, packages, weights, and marks & numbers.

HS6 & description checks

Enforces 6-digit HS format and flags vague terms in goods descriptions; encourages clear, specific item wording.

Party/address completeness

Prompts for buyer/seller/consignee details with street, city, postal code, and country to meet structured address expectations.

Validation log & score

Runs a full audit, writes line-item issues to a log, and shows a completeness score to guide fixes before export.

CSV export

Exports master/house data in a carrier-neutral CSV layout that can be uploaded to carrier portals or filing systems.

Least-privilege by design

Operates on the current spreadsheet only; no external servers; no access to other Drive files.

How it works

  1. Install the add-on from the Google Workspace Marketplace and open a Google Sheet.
  2. Generate templates (master/house) and fill data for each shipment line.
  3. Run checks to highlight missing or low-quality fields (HS6, descriptions, addresses, weights, UN/LOCODE shape, etc.).
  4. Export CSV and upload to your carrier or ENS filing platform.

Who benefits: freight forwarders, BCOs, 3PLs, carriers, and customs teams preparing ENS data under ICS2.

FAQ

Does this file the ENS to EU customs?

No. The add-on prepares high-quality data files. Filing is done by your carrier, customs broker, or platform.

Which transport modes?

Designed for maritime, inland waterways, road, and rail ENS data preparation aligned with ICS2 timelines.

What about HS codes and descriptions?

It validates 6-digit HS format and encourages complete, specific commercial descriptions to reduce rejections.
